Stub only — the full omnibox lives in specimens/omnibox.html. This exploration is about where the trigger sits.

Viewport 1280px — hamburger, drawer, and search trigger are clickable in every option.

Option A — Current + Search (baseline)

Today's header, faithfully: slate bar, the crest hanging over the hero, uppercase Goudy links, gold Donate — with the omnibox pill slotted beside the logo. The control group every redesign must beat.

Optimizes for
Zero migration risk and total familiarity. The overlapping-crest signature is untouched, and the omnibox lands without moving a single link.
Trade-offs
Nav type runs 11–13px uppercase — below the site's own 14px floor, and six links + pill + crest all fight for one row. Search reads as an accessory, not a feature.
Where search lives
Desktop: labeled pill (icon + "Search" + /) just right of the crest. Tablet/mobile: icon-only circle beside the hamburger — it never moves into the drawer.
Mobile answer
Crest shrinks to 104px; nav collapses to the left-slide slate drawer (Donate inside). Search stays surfaced as the icon circle in the bar.
Sticky on scroll
Nothing — matching production, the whole header scrolls away. The / shortcut is the only persistent route to search.

Option B — Two-Tier Split

A darker utility tray (search center, News · Contact left, Donate right) sits above a calmer brand bar carrying the crest and the three primary destinations at a legible 15px. Wayfinding and utility each get their own floor.

Optimizes for
Search becomes permanently discoverable — a wide, field-shaped trigger in its own tray (the cb-fish dark-tray move, recast in slate + gold). Primary nav finally breathes.
Trade-offs
Roughly 44px more chrome before the hero. Two dark bars must stay visually distinct (the tray is darkened slate with a hairline), and Donate is less commanding at tray scale.
Where search lives
Desktop/tablet: the wide tray trigger, centered. Mobile: the tray flips below the brand bar as a full-width search bar — persistent, thumb-reachable.
Mobile answer
Brand bar = crest + hamburger; the drawer groups primary (large) over News/Contact (small) with Donate at the foot. Search keeps its own full-width row.
Sticky on scroll
Tray only — a slim 44px strip with search and Donate follows you; the brand bar scrolls away with the page.

Option C — Centered Logo

An ivory hairline utility row (search + Donate) above a slate bar where the crest sits dead-center between split nav, dropping into the hero like a crest on a building facade. This is a hall of fame — let it feel like the institution it is.

Optimizes for
Ceremony and symmetry. The crest gains scale and prominence; the warm gold-50 utility strip frames the dark bar like museum letterhead.
Trade-offs
Split nav locks you into a 2/3 link balance — adding a seventh link later gets awkward. Three stacked strips cost vertical room, and centered logos are slower wayfinding than left-anchored ones.
Where search lives
Desktop: light pill in the utility row, top right. Tablet/mobile: the utility row drops; search becomes an icon circle mirroring the hamburger across the centered crest.
Mobile answer
Symmetric bar — hamburger left, crest center, search icon right. Donate moves into the drawer as the gold button.
Sticky on scroll
None by default. Recommended: a condensed sticky reprise (56px inline crest + hamburger + search icon) that fades in after the full header scrolls off.

Option D — Search-Forward

The Hall is a database of people — so the search field itself, not a pill, is the header's center of gravity. Brand condenses to a compact lockup; nav condenses to Hall of Famers + More ▾ + Donate. Treat search as the front door.

Optimizes for
The dominant visitor job: "find a player." A 560px field makes the omnibox impossible to miss — the reference-site pattern (IMDb, Baseball-Reference) applied to ultimate.
Trade-offs
Selection Process, About, News, and Contact lose top-level visibility behind More. A field this prominent promises instant answers, so the omnibox must be fast — and the bar is the busiest of the five.
Where search lives
Everywhere, always: a real input. Desktop centers it; tablet lets it flex between lockup and hamburger; focusing it opens the omnibox overlay.
Mobile answer
Two rows — crest + hamburger on top, then a persistent full-width search bar. Search never hides behind an icon; Donate and all links move to the drawer.
Sticky on scroll
The entire compact header sticks — it is the search bar, so hiding it would defeat the concept.

Option E — Masthead (my pick to argue about)

A broadsheet nameplate: centered crest above the wordmark set big in Goudy italic, with a faint gold glow — then one slim everything-bar (all five links + search + Donate) beneath a gold hairline. Identity gets its ceremony once; a single slim bar does all the work forever after.

Optimizes for
The arrival moment. No current option ever shows the institution's name in chrome — the crest alone carries it. And after first paint, the chrome that follows you is the leanest of all five options.
Trade-offs
Tallest first-paint chrome (~190px desktop). The wordmark would collide with a hero that repeats the name — homepage hero copy should shift to a tagline (shown here). Interior pages likely want the slim bar alone.
Where search lives
In the slim bar, far right, always: labeled pill on desktop and tablet, icon circle at mobile. Because the bar is the sticky element, search is permanently one tap away.
Mobile answer
Masthead compresses to a 60px crest + 22px wordmark; the bar keeps hamburger + search icon + compact Donate. Nothing important ever leaves the surface.
Sticky on scroll
Slim bar only — the nameplate scrolls away like a newspaper masthead, leaving a 52px strip with nav, search, and Donate.

Scores, option, and viewport persist locally (uh-header-exploration). Esc closes drawers, menus, and the search stub.
